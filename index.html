Welcome to My Siegel+Gale Interview

This interactive presentation explores my design philosophy through the lens of mathematical symbols—addition, multiplication, and equals—which mirror Siegel+Gale's commitment to simplification.

Each section represents a key stage in the design process, from gathering complexity to achieving clarity, highlighting my approach to creating meaningful, user-centered experiences that align with Siegel+Gale's core values.

Navigation: Use mouse scroll, keyboard arrows, or the progress dots at the bottom to move between sections. Click "View Gallery" to see examples of my work.

Be sure to navigate to the end for an interactive game experience!

+ (Addition)

Designing for Humans, Not Just Clients

Design begins with empathy. My background in HCI and user research informs every decision—placing people before pixels, users before aesthetics. I craft experiences that feel intuitive, effortless, and true to their needs.

Example: EasyWith's Momentium Museum (User-Centered UX Work)

× (Multiplication)

Bridging Disciplines, Expanding Impact

Great design is never singular. It thrives in the intersections—between research and storytelling, strategy and visuals, ideas and execution. I bring together diverse skillsets to create cohesive, impactful brand experiences.

Example: TEDXRISD

= (Equals)

Simplicity is the Ultimate Future

The clearest ideas endure. A brand distilled to its essence is timeless, unmistakable. I strip away the noise, refining complex systems into seamless interactions—design that is as functional as it is beautiful.

Example: Fictional Redesign Challenge (e.g., Government Portal, Healthcare Scheduling, or Financial Dashboard)
